A&F Outerwear Visual Filters

Project Goals

Tested variations of outerwear visual filters with current/potential A&F shoppers to gain qualitative insight on how users prioritize attributess when shopping for outerwear, and if this changes between fashion/functional outerwear.

Methodology

Remote, unmoderated, task-based user study + questionaire

Participants

15 current/potential A&F shoppers

All participants were recruited via IntelliZoom, the participant community of our user research platform, UserZoom.

Demographics

  • 8 female, 7 male
  • US only
  • Shops for clothes online at least seasonally
  • Age 22-29
  • College educated at a 4-year university (current student or recent graduate)
  • Shops within the A&F competitive set

Results

What is the customer mindset when shopping online for outerwear?

Most users indicated they start their shopping expereince with at least a general idea of what they're looking for, but not necessarily a specific garment in mind.

Users are not as likely to approach outerwear shopping with intention to just browse until they find something they like.

What attributes are most important when shopping for fashion outerwear?

From the list provided, users prioritized color, style, and warmth for fashion outerwear shopping.

What attributes are most important when shopping for functional outerwear?

From the list provided, users prioritized warmth and mashine washability for functional outerwear shopping, with secondary/tertiary priority spread amoung several attributes.

Filter utility

Users were able to easily naviagte and understand the filter tabs, but a few weren't sure how to interpret warmth buckets.

"Confusing where warm scale was starting; filter said coats which makes me think it's winter specific but it's on the coats and jackets page and there were light jackets so maybe warm started with spring jacket warmth?"

Features icons and descriptions were rated very clear (4.7/5)

Warmth tab

Users preferred the gradient scale over icons or checkboxes, commenting that the warm hues help communicate the warmth of the garment.

"The warm color palette used here signals feelings of increasing warmth as I look from left to right on the scale."

"Showing the color range gives a better idea about the warmth level of the coat and design-wise looks better with the added color."

There is a notable user appetite for temperature ratings.

Shop by style

Users were receptive of the 'shop by style' concept, but 73% of users would prefer to shop by color/warmth/features.

"I would appreciate this option and I like this feature, but i'd rather have the other feature available to use too"

"If the brand sticks largely to certain styles it makes sense. If they have a large array/variance of style then sorting the other way on the previos page makes more sense."

However, users predict their preferences would change in different scenarios.

- By style for fashion outwear, or if shopping for something specific

"If I already has a winter jacket that suited my weather needs, and simply wanted another one for the aesthetics, then I would shop by style."
