Connect for Health Colorado – Suggested Plan Renewals UX Research

Project Overview

At Connect for Health Colorado, the renewals process plays a crucial role in ensuring individuals and families maintain continuous health coverage. However, our research revealed that users faced confusion, anxiety, and uncertainty throughout the renewal experience, especially when interacting with the suggested plan renewals process.

As the UX Researcher on this project, my goal was to identify friction points, uncover user expectations, and enhance the Suggested Plans feature for a more transparent, intuitive, and reassuring renewal experience.

Research Approach & Methodology

We conducted moderated user interviews with eight Connect for Health renewals users, including:

  • 6 self-service users navigating the renewal process independently
  • 2 broker-assisted users working with professionals for plan selection

Each 45-minute to 1-hour session focused on understanding:

  • How users interacted with Suggested Plan Renewals
  • What information and support they needed to make decisions
  • Pain points that caused frustration or confusion

Key Findings & Insights

Notices & Communication Anxiety

  • Users received renewal notices via email and mail, but they often skimmed or ignored them.
  • Many felt stressed and overwhelmed by the process, balancing renewal decisions with personal responsibilities.
  • Some proactively renewed, while others waited until the last minute, risking lapses in coverage.

Opportunity: Improve notice design and messaging to ease anxiety, highlight key actions, and provide clear next steps.

Welcome Page

Many users didn't seem to remember the welcome page that much indicating that they don't or didn't interact with it much. There was confusion on the expected functionality of the CTAs on the page with users giving mixed responses on what would happen if they were to select one. The copy of the page was unclear and there was a comment on the color of the open enrollment.

View Application

  • View application was seen as a place to view all current and past applications by some users. One user thought that 'View Application' was where they could make changes to their application.

Apply for 2024/Make Changes

  • 'Make changes' and 'Apply for 2024' wasn't clear to users. They weren't sure if it meant that they were changing personal information, their plans, or their dependents.

Opportunities:

  • Rework the welcome page into a dashboard with a clear renewal status, personalized recommendations, and next steps.
  • Use explicit labeling to guide users on what actions they are taking.

Suggested Plans Page

  • Users didn’t understand why a particular plan was suggested.
  • Some thought cost was the primary factor, but they still researched on their own for better coverage.
  • Many were frustrated when they found their old plan (or a similar one) but weren’t offered it as a suggestion.
  • Users wanted comparison tools to see what changed from their old plan vs. suggested plan.
  • The ‘Cancel Suggested Plan’ CTA was unclear—users hesitated, unsure of its effect.

    • Opportunities:

    • Provide clear reasoning for plan suggestions, alternative options, and an easy comparison tool.
    • Rename the 'Cancel Suggested Plan' action to something more intuitive and reassuring.

Income & Tax Credit Adjustments

  • Self-employed users struggled to adjust projected income and do tax credit math on their own.
  • Navigating back to plan options after income updates was a challenge.

Opportunity: Create guided income adjustment flows with real-time tax credit estimates.
